<p><strong>The MGS Student Leadership Programme supports the school vision of building a caring and enabling school environment through nurturing a sense of belonging, appreciating diversity, empowering learners, and monitoring and supporting well-being.&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p>The Student Leaders play an important role in building an MGS culture
where all areas of need are met, so that every member owns the community
and contributes to building it up.&nbsp; They do this by being and becoming:</p>
<p></p>
<p><a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1smFrBC6QwvA7II7tKmLWl512grOu6C8u/view?usp=drive_link" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>flower v3 09102024.png</u></a>
</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Enablers who encourage deeper ownership and accountability towards the
MGS community</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Connectors who forge enduring relationships amongst the MGS community</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Nurturers who foster a deep sense of belonging within the MGS community</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Catalysts of positive well-being in the MGS community</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Student Leaders must consistently demonstrate a leadership posture that
is consistent in role-modelling the school values of Godliness, Excellence
and Love. Opportunities through the various school platforms will be provided
for them to demonstrate their leadership skills. The Student Leadership
teachers journey with them in the process to support their growth.</p>
<p><strong><u>Leadership Boards</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Students who demonstrate strong leadership potential and a commitment
to serving the school community are selected to join one of three leadership
boards, where they undergo further development in leadership knowledge,
skills, and competencies. The three Leadership Boards work in partnership
to organise and execute major school-wide events such Orientation, MG Week
and Teachers’ Day.</p>
